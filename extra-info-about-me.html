Extra info about me 

"How I Found My Passion for Taking Photos of the Sky"

It didn’t start with a fancy camera or any kind of plan — just a moment. One evening, I looked up and saw the sky painted in colors I couldn’t describe. It was a sunset that didn’t just fade behind the horizon — it spoke to me. I pulled out my phone, snapped a picture, and that small moment changed something.

After that, I started paying more attention to the sky — how it shifted during the day, how clouds formed shapes, how each sunset was never the same as the last. I realized that nature creates art every single day, and

 all I had to do was notice it. So I did — and I started

 capturing it.

Photography became more than just clicking a button. It became a way to slow down, to connect with the world around me, and to express how I see beauty — especially in things others might overlook. Clouds, golden light, the quiet before dusk — they became my favorite subjects.

That’s how I found my passion: by noticing the beauty in everyday moments and wanting to hold onto them — even just for a second longer.
